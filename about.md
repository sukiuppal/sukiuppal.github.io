I am a psychodynamic psychotherapist and CBT therapist with over 20 years of experience supporting children and adults across a wide range of mental health settings. My clinical background includes roles at the Priory Hospital, the NHS, and Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services (CAMHS), where I have drawn on integrative training to deliver thoughtful, evidence-based care.

Alongside my clinical work, I have contributed to the charity sector through psychodynamic psychotherapy — including serving as a befriender with Age UK, a volunteer child counsellor with Place2Be, and a volunteer psychotherapist at a London-based charity for adults. These experiences have deepened my commitment to making mental health support accessible to all.

Throughout my career I have had the privilege of working with people from all walks of life and across all age groups, many navigating difficult life events that give rise to depression, anxiety, low self-esteem, and more. I am currently studying a foundation course in group analysis at the Institute of Group Analysis, exploring how sibling and family dynamics shape our experiences in group situations.

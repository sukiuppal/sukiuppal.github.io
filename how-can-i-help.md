Whatever brings you here, you do not need to have everything figured out before reaching out. Many people come to therapy feeling unsure of exactly what they need — and that is completely okay. Together, we can explore what is going on and find the right way forward.

## Areas I work with

I have experience supporting people through a wide range of difficulties, including:

- **Depression** — persistent low mood, loss of motivation, feelings of hopelessness
- **Anxiety** — worry, panic, social anxiety, generalised anxiety
- **Low self-esteem** — negative self-image, self-criticism, people-pleasing
- **Trauma** — past or recent experiences that continue to affect daily life
- **Anger** — difficulties managing strong emotions or understanding their roots
- **Sexuality** — questions around sexual identity, orientation, or relationships
- **Identity** — questions of who you are, where you belong, or what you want from life
- **Relationship difficulties** — patterns in relationships, communication, attachment
- **Grief and loss** — bereavement, endings, major life transitions
- **Work and life stress** — burnout, pressure, lack of direction

## If you are from a Black or Asian background

If you are living with anxiety, depression, or low self-esteem, and you are a man or woman from a Black or Asian background, you may find that these experiences are shaped by pressures that go unspoken — or unrecognised by those around you.

You may be experiencing:

- Struggling with family and cultural expectations
- Questions about your cultural identity
- Feeling anxious with the amount of responsibility you carry
- Struggling to find your place and belonging
- Living a double life, whilst judging what you desire and what is expected from you
- Confusing feelings about your sexuality

These are struggles which may be invisible expectations that you carry around within yourself. They can lead to feelings of depression, isolation, anger, panic attacks and anxiety, but you keep finding yourself just getting on with things. While you may be experiencing relationship breakdowns and feeling misunderstood by those around you.

These are not background details. They are central to who you are and how you experience the world.

I take an intersectional approach, which means I don't separate the personal from the political or the individual from their context. Whether you are navigating intergenerational trauma, faith-based expectations, experiences of marginalisation, or simply the pressure of being the one in your family who is supposed to hold it all together, I will meet you with curiosity, care and cultural humility.

I am LGBTQ+ affirming and neurodivergent-friendly, and I am committed to creating a therapy space where difference is not just tolerated but genuinely understood.

## Ready to reach out?

You may feel torn — part of you wanting to try therapy, while another part holds back. Thoughts like *"I'll be okay"*, *"I don't have the time"*, or *"others have it worse than me"* are incredibly common, and often part of how we protect ourselves from vulnerability, particularly if you lean toward an avoidant attachment style.

I understand that attachment styles play a vital role in how we form relationships, and many of us have an avoidant attachment style:

- Finding it harder to trust others
- The need to be self-reliant
- Avoiding committing to long-term relationships
- Struggling to name your emotions and feeling disconnected

This can make reaching out for therapy very difficult. You may be wondering if anyone would really understand your struggles. But the fact that you're here, reading this, says something important. There is a part of you that is ready. I'd encourage you to trust that part.

Therapy can offer something you may not have had before: a private, honest space that is entirely yours, free from expectation. I will meet you with a gentle and accepting approach, deepening my connection to your unique experience, allowing you to share something that feels immensely difficult or painful. I will hear your story without blame or judgement. I believe that the therapeutic relationship is vital to building trust at your pace.

Taking the first step can feel difficult. If you're considering it, feel free to reach out for a free consultation call — no pressure, no commitment. Just a conversation.
